OUR MISSION:

We, Good Trouble, as college students, aspire to inspire, teach and influence middle and high school students, creating a pathway of success for them.

Highschool Program

  • In class sessions – In class session will consist of in-class discussions about various topics that will help students make a successful transition from High School to college, and to continue that success. (i.e. goals, grants, leadership, Pick Your Path, bullying, scholarships, time management, resume building, communication/speech skills, selecting a college, loans, interview techniques, branding yourself, networking, organizations, peer pressure, volunteering, money management, how to study/note-taking in high school & middle school etc.)

  • After school tutoring – college students will assist teachers in after school tutoring/homework help.

  • High school Ambassadors - selected high school students along with Good Trouble, will go into middle schools and help with in class sessions. High school ambassadors will also help MS students to understand the transition from MS to HS. This will allow the students to practice communication/speech skills as well as exercising their leadership abilities. Students can apply to be Ambassadors by application, resume and a final interview.

  • Mentor Match Weekend

    • Mentee will spend one night on campus with Mentor. Mentee will get a tour of the campus and eat on campus. She/he will also meet with a professor or admissions advisor at the university.

    • Mentor and mentee will study together, going over any homework that the mentee has or needs help understanding

    • Mentor and mentee will spend a Saturday morning volunteering at/with a pre-determined location or organization.

  • Friday Fun - Monthly, Good Trouble will host an educational yet fun event, organized by Good Trouble students. These events are open to all students.
